I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Michigan's School of Information and School of School Work, with research interests in computational social science, housing loss, socioeconomic shocks, social policy, and mental health. My advisors are Daniel Romero and Kristin Seefeldt. I have also collaborated extensively with David Jurgens (School of Information) and Bri Mezuk (School of Public Health). Before starting my Ph.D., I worked in the social sector for over 5 years, including serving as the Research and Development Director at fintech nonprofit Mission Asset Fund and co-founding and serving as Shelter Director of Heart and Home Collaborative Women's Shelter. This experience has been critical in my community-engaged scholarship. I still work with Mission Asset Fund as a research consultant on their guaranteed income trial for immigrant families, and with Heart and Home on their Board of Directors and as the program director for their new Overnight Warming Location in partnership with the City of Palo Alto, CA. Before that, I got my Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics and Master's Degree in Management Science and Engineering (Operations Research and Analytics) from Stanford University.
